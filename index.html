About

About Forward Vision

Forward Vision Football was set up in 2019 to support young footballers striving to improve themselves. With coaching that is personal and tailored to the athlete, it’s a place where you can be pushed towards reaching your potential. After being involved in many different coaching clubs and experiencing first had how teams train, from grass roots level through to professional academies (such as Newcastle united and Harrogate Town), it is clear to see that some parts of training and coaching are hard to execute in a large team environment.

This is where forward vision comes in, offering additional training that is specifically aimed to improve the athlete incorporating your aims and objectives whether that is to become fitter, faster, stronger, get you into your first team and beyond that. At Forward Vision Football the clue is in the name, we want people to start looking forward and to realise their potential in themselves, yes, a lot of players all want to be a professional footballer but lets first find out how to enable you to reach your full potential and see how far that takes you.

Forward Vision Football aims to work alongside your weekly team training sessions and matches providing that additional training to work on what you want, guided by the coach, to help develop your game to perform at your best in game.

Classes

What to Expect

Ages 8 and under

For the younger age group, U9s, it's all about mastering the fundamental movements, we will work on the sport’s specific movements for football and many other sports. This is key at this age and is something this age group will be developing at school, because without being able to control how you move, how can you be expected to control a football? Players will develop their ABC’s (Agility, Balance and Coordination) during sessions alongside the football training in order to make them more effective footballers and rounded young sports persons. On our newsfeed you will see videos of how we do this in sessions.

Ages 8-13

For the young adolescent age groups, players will be focusing on skill mastery, both with and without the football. A big key to football is speed and a lot of our training is focused on creating the match tempo high intensity within the session to improve this element. Control and manipulation of the football is now key, still working alongside the mastery of fundamental movements, we will progress to working on effective:

  • Dribbling (Touches, speed and creativity)
  • Passing (all types and techniques/accuracy)
  • Shooting (Power, accuracy and precision)
The mastery of the fundamental movements and specifically the ABC’s will support the players development with the football, enabling things like faster turns, quicker sprints and tighter touches.

Ages 13+

As we advance upwards past 13, training is specific to how the player has developed over the past years as we look to make them stronger, faster and fitter (Cardiovascular fitness/Stamina). By using body weight training and HIIT training we will be able to improve physical fitness to enable the player to perform more effectively on the pitch. A lot of work will be done to continue to improve ball work and technique alongside specific technical and tactical drills which will be tailored to the players playing position.

Advanced Players

For some players who are more developed, strength and conditioning can be used for those who want to get fitter, healthier and train towards that athlete physique. Training will be fun and rewarding and it will be great for everyone associated with the participant to see the improvements we can make.

Inclusivity

Finally, Forward Vision Football is a platform for all, its all-inclusive, no matter what size, shape, ethnicity or gender you are you will always be welcome and treated the same because at the end of the day everyone is training for the same reason. To better themselves and enjoy working hard.

About Joe

As a youngster I grew up with a love for football, played at development and academy level from the age of 12 through to the age of 17 at various clubs including; Rotherham United, Chesterfield FC and Harrogate town along with a trial at Leicester City at the age of 15. During my time at Chesterfield I was able to travel to the Netherlands and Barcelona for Youth Academy tournaments. As I transitioned out of football due to a string of injuries I began to start coaching.

I am now 20 years old and studying Sports Coaching at Northumbria University, alongside working with two grass roots teams: Westgate JFC Newcastle and New Park Bulldogs Harrogate. I am currently an F.A Level 2 qualified coach along with a 1st4sport Level 2 qualification in coaching multi-skills development in sport which is very beneficial for coaching younger ages groups as it is important to coach the fundamental movements that are crucial for football.

The course I study specialises in Coaching science (Psychology, Biomechanics, physiology and nutrition, Developing athlete potential and coaching skills and practice. Therefore, I am up to date with the latest findings and strategies to coach and develop players to a good standard. I am keen to continue developing as a coach and am due to start coaching with the Newcastle United Foundation for the forthcoming year. In addition to this I want to peruse an interest in fitness and undertake a HIIT (High intensity interval training) training course alongside a specialist nutrition course to develop my knowledge in this field.
